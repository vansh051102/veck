# VECK Sales SOP Guide (for the sales team)

The CRM enforces VECK's 4-step sales process. You cannot skip steps — the system blocks stage changes until the step's requirements are met.

## The 4 steps

### Step 1 — NEW LEAD (respond within 1 hour)

Every new lead automatically gets two checklists:

- **New Lead Registration** (4 items): customer name, company, contact number, email.
- **Initial Contact** (4 items): catalogue sent, brochure sent, intro message, call attempt #1.

To move the lead to **Contacted** you must complete both checklists **and** log at least 1 activity (your first call/WhatsApp/email).

### Step 2 — CONTACTED (qualify within 24 hours)

On entering Contacted the system creates:

- **Contacted Qualification** (22 items): customer type, decision maker, product, quantity, specs (size/thickness/grade/…), transport, payment, budget.
- **Lead Validation** (5 items): serviceable location, viable quantity, we supply the product, genuine requirement, commercially viable.

To move to **Qualified** you must complete both checklists and have at least 3 activities logged in total.

### Step 3 — QUALIFIED (hand over within 3 hours)

The **Qualified Checklist** (17 items) covers verifying details, sharing the complete enquiry with Purchase, getting acknowledgement, verifying and sending the quotation, and updating the CRM. Complete it, create the quote from the lead page, then move to **Quote Sent**.

### Step 4 — QUOTE SENT (follow up daily for 6 days)

Moving to Quote Sent automatically schedules **6 daily follow-up tasks** on the lead. Do the follow-up call each day and log it. Missed follow-ups show as **Overdue** on the lead timeline (checked hourly). The **Quote Sent Checklist** (10 items) tracks receipt confirmation, feedback, objections, and the final outcome.

Exit: **Closed Won**, or **Deal Lost** with a reason.

## Losing a deal

Moving a lead to **Deal Lost** or **Disqualified** requires picking a reason from the fixed list (Purchased Elsewhere, No Requirement, Project Cancelled, Price Not Accepted, Delivery Timeline Not Accepted, Payment Terms Not Accepted, Product Not Suitable, No Response, Requirement Postponed, Budget Issue, Credit Requirement Issue, Dormant). Free text is not accepted — this keeps loss analysis meaningful.

Terminal stages (Closed Won / Deal Lost / Disqualified) are final: leads there cannot be moved or deleted.

## SLA timers

Each stage has a deadline: New Lead **1 hour**, Contacted **24 hours**, Qualified **3 hours**, Quote Sent **6 days**. Breached leads are flagged red in the leads table — clear your breaches first thing every day.

## Day-to-day tips

- The leads page has stage tabs, a Kanban view, quick 7/30/90-day filters, and bulk assign — use "Assign to me" to grab a lead.
- Change stage/priority/assignee directly in the table row; forward stage moves only (losses need a reason, so they go through the lead page).
- Log every customer touch as an activity — calls need duration and outcome, meetings need attendees and time. The system counts activities when deciding whether you can advance the lead.
- Import leads via the CSV **Import** button (Company, First Name, Last Name, Email, Phone columns); export the current view with **Export**.
